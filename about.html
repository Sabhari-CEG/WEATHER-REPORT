Weather is the state of the atmosphere, describing for example the degree to which it is hot or cold, wet or dry, calm or stormy, clear or cloudy.

Let us see some of the weather below




SUNNY


Sunny weather days are days when there are very little or no clouds in the sky. We usually experience more sunny days in the summer when the days are warmer.



CLOUDY


When the sky is cloudy, it's so full of clouds that you can't see the sun.You can also describe an issue, thought, or memory as cloudy when it's vague or unclear.



THUNDER


Thunderstorm, a violent short-lived weather disturbance that is almost always associated with lightning, thunder, dense clouds, heavy rain or hail, and strong gusty winds.



FOG


Fog is a cloud that touches the ground. Fog can be thin or thick, and difficult to see through it.Fog shows up when water vapor, or water in its gaseous form, condenses.



SHOWER


showery is a weather with light rain that is often not continuous.Especially prevalent on dull showery days, but was found during the period immediately after the rains.



DOWNPOUR


A downpour is a sudden and unexpected heavy fall of rain.Also defined as a lot of rain falling in a short time.



SUNSHOWER


A sunshower or sun shower is a meteorological phenomenon in which rain falls while the sun is shining.It is usually the result of accompanying winds and rain storm.



SNOW


Snow comprises individual ice crystals that grow while suspended in the atmosphere within clouds and then fall, accumulating on the ground.
